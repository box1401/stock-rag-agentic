# 2330 TSMC — Long thesis (March 2026 update)

## Investment thesis (TL;DR)
TSMC remains the structurally privileged supplier of leading-edge logic
silicon for the AI accelerator cycle. Pricing power on N3 / N3E and the
ramp of N2 GAA in late 2026 should expand gross margin into the 58–60%
band by 4Q26, even after factoring in fab CapEx for Arizona Phase 2 and
Kumamoto Phase 3.

## Demand drivers
- **Hyperscaler accelerator demand.** Hyperscaler order books for
  custom ASICs (Google TPU, Meta MTIA, Amazon Trainium2) have multi-quarter
  visibility through 2027.
- **Apple SoC migration to N2.** A-series and M-series moving to N2 in
  late 2026 reinforces wafer pricing.
- **Auto + Edge AI.** Slow but steady N5/N4 demand from automotive ECUs
  and on-device inference SoCs maintains mature-node utilization.

## Risks (must monitor)
1. **Geopolitical.** Cross-strait tension or US export-control escalation
   on advanced nodes could trigger order pull-ins and subsequent inventory
   correction.
2. **CapEx digestion.** 2026 CapEx is guided at USD 38–42B; any delay in
   AI demand would compress free cash flow and raise the dividend cover.
3. **Yield ramp on N2.** Defect density on N2 must stay on schedule;
   any 1–2 quarter slip would compress 2H27 ASP power.

## Valuation reference
- 10-yr forward PE band: 12x (trough) — 28x (peak), median 18x.
- Below 14x forward = clearly attractive given AI cycle still intact.
- Above 25x forward = priced for perfection; trim accordingly.

## Position sizing rule
- Core position 6–8% of equity book.
- Add on a >15% drawdown if PE drops below 16x forward.
- Trim on PE > 24x forward or any single-quarter gross margin miss > 200 bps.
